Lightweight Ankle Exoskeleton

The lightweight ankle exoskeleton project was complemented by a research paper analysing its efficacy. Presented at ICBT Cardiff 2025, it will be published by SpringerNature, a widely-reputed Swiss publisher, under Studies in Systems, Decisions, and Control. The ankle exoskeleton reduced jerk accelerations near the ankle from peaks of magnitude 3 m/s^2 to peaks of magnitude 0.75 m/s^2, measured using an MPU6050 accelerometer.

Autorotation of Maple seeds

Maple seeds autorotate, spin rapidly without any power, because of how they're shaped. This is an evolutionary adaption to optimise seed dispersal. Autorotation is a promising application of biomimicry that can be used to optimise wind-turbines. My CREST report focused on how their size and tensile strength affects autorotation using origami models. The project was awarded CREST Gold.

Aspect ratio of aerofoils

My IB Physics Extended Essay investigated the impact of aspect ratio on the lift, drag, and overall efficiency of aerofoils. It used a Computational Fluid Dynamics simulation at low-glider flying conditions. The final result, suggesting an optimum aspect ratio of 8.85 is close to approximate aspect ratio 9 used by Boeing aircrafts.
